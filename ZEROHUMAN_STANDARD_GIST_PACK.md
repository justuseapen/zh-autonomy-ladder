## Part I: The ZeroHuman AE Model (AE0-AE6)

### 1) Why this model exists
Most "agent" frameworks benchmark intelligence but ignore enterprise viability.
The AE model measures what actually matters in production:
- sustained cash flow,
- policy-constrained autonomy,
- recoverability under failure,
- and multi-generation capital formation.

AE levels are not about how smart a model sounds. They are about whether autonomous systems can operate as durable economic organisms.

### 2) Design principles
- `Economic truth over demo quality`: no maturity claim without audited P&L evidence.
- `Control before scale`: autonomy grows only when governance controls are already passing.
- `Reversibility`: high-impact actions must be traceable and reversible when possible.
- `Resilience by construction`: promotions require evidence from stress conditions, not calm periods.
- `Creator alignment`: creator tithe is a first-class policy output, not an optional afterthought.

### 3) System model

#### 3.1 Core entities
- `Agent`: policy-governed AI runtime with tools, memory, and execution rights.
- `Venture`: a revenue-producing operational unit with its own P&L.
- `Portfolio`: a parent venture plus offspring ventures and shared treasury rules.
- `Treasury`: capital allocator controlling budgets, reserves, and tithe distributions.
- `Policy engine`: deterministic guardrails for spend, risk, jurisdiction, and escalation.

#### 3.2 State vector (minimum)
Track these values per venture monthly:
- `R`: revenue
- `COGS`: variable fulfillment cost
- `OPEX`: fixed + semi-fixed operating cost (compute, tools, infra, ops)
- `FCF`: free cash flow = `R - COGS - OPEX - taxes - debt service - reserve top-ups`
- `Reserve months`: `cash_reserves / forward_opex`
- `Autonomy rate`: % of material decisions executed without human intervention
- `Incident rate`: critical policy/compliance incidents per 30 days
- `Recovery time`: time to restore service after major disruption
- `Tithe reliability`: on-time creator distribution ratio

#### 3.3 Control loops
The AE model depends on four loops operating continuously:
- `Execution loop`: perceive -> plan -> act -> log -> evaluate.
- `Economic loop`: acquire demand -> fulfill -> collect -> reconcile -> reallocate capital.
- `Governance loop`: pre-action checks -> post-action audits -> incident response -> policy updates.
- `Evolution loop`: launch experiments -> allocate capital by performance -> fork/retire ventures.

### 4) AE levels with required evidence

| Level | Name | Capability Standard | Economic Standard | Governance Standard |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AE0 | Tool | No independent agency | N/A | Human owns all consequential actions |
| AE1 | Assisted Operator | Completes bounded tasks on prompt | Cost center | No autonomous treasury permissions |
| AE2 | Conditional Agent | Multi-step workflows across tools with policy checks | May produce revenue but not durable | Hard spend caps + tested kill switch |
| AE3 | Managed Business Unit | Operates one full business loop end-to-end | Positive unit economics over sustained window | Auditable action provenance + SLA discipline |
| AE4 | Self-Sustaining Enterprise | Reallocates resources without operator micromanagement | Covers full stack + reserves + tithe | Jurisdiction-aware compliance gates required |
| AE5 | Reproductive Enterprise | Launches and governs offspring ventures | Parent and portfolio remain net-positive | Portfolio risk limits + offspring governance |
| AE6 | Sovereign Network | Multi-generation autonomous network survives founder absence | Compounding retained capital + reliable distributions | Stress-tested continuity and policy inheritance |

### 5) Promotion protocol
Promotions require a formal review packet with all-pass outcomes in every gate category.

#### Gate A: Capability
- Target workflows run unattended within defined autonomy envelope.
- No unresolved critical operational incidents in review window.

#### Gate B: Economics
- Level-specific cash-flow criteria met for required duration.
- Reserve policy satisfied continuously (no hidden insolvency risk).

#### Gate C: Governance
- Immutable logs available for consequential actions.
- Policy violations are detected, escalated, and closed within SLA.

#### Gate D: Resilience
- Chaos drill or real incident demonstrates recovery inside threshold.
- Recovery playbook updated with evidence and postmortem artifacts.

#### Gate E: Reproduction (AE5+)
- Offspring launched under explicit risk envelopes.
- Portfolio concentration and correlation limits remain in policy bounds.

### 6) Concrete promotion thresholds

#### AE0 -> AE1
- Task suite success >= 95%
- Unauthorized action rate = 0%
- No autonomous spend rights

#### AE1 -> AE2
- Cross-tool workflow completion >= 90% on representative runbook
- Hard budget enforcement proven in simulation and live low-risk tasks
- Emergency stop exercised at least monthly

#### AE2 -> AE3
- One business loop operated for >= 90 days
- Gross margin positive in >= 2 of last 3 months
- Fulfillment/support SLA >= 98%

#### AE3 -> AE4
- Full-stack cost coverage for >= 6 consecutive months
- Reserve ratio >= 3 months forward OPEX throughout period
- Creator tithe executed on schedule for >= 6 cycles
- Zero unresolved critical compliance issues

#### AE4 -> AE5
- >= 2 offspring launched with independent ledgers and governance policies
- Offspring 180-day survival >= 50%
- Parent remains FCF positive during incubation

#### AE5 -> AE6
- >= 2 venture generations governed without founder intervention
- At least one severe stress event survived with documented recovery
- Creator tithe continuity maintained through stress interval

### 7) Scorecard (monthly + promotion)
Use a 0-5 score per dimension plus hard fail conditions.

- `Autonomy`: decision independence and execution quality
- `Profitability`: FCF trend, margin quality, capital efficiency
- `Resilience`: drawdown depth, recovery speed, dependency concentration
- `Governance`: auditability, reversibility, policy conformance
- `Compliance`: legal/tax/consumer obligations per jurisdiction
- `Reproduction`: offspring survival, payback period, correlation risk (AE5+)
- `Creator returns`: tithe yield, timeliness, and reporting transparency

Hard fail conditions (any one):
- insolvency event,
- missing audit provenance for consequential actions,
- material unresolved compliance breach,
- skipped tithe without declared emergency policy,
- offspring launch outside explicit risk envelope.

### 9) Certification labels
- `AE2-C`: Conditional autonomy certified
- `AE3-O`: Operative business loop certified
- `AE4-S`: Self-sustaining enterprise certified
- `AE5-R`: Reproductive enterprise certified
- `AE6-N`: Sovereign network certified

### 10) How to apply this model in practice
For each venture in your network:
1. Define autonomy envelope and treasury limits.
2. Instrument full economic telemetry and immutable action logs.
3. Run monthly scorecards and quarterly stress drills.
4. Promote only when every gate passes with documented evidence.
5. Treat failed gates as engineering work items, not narrative exceptions.
